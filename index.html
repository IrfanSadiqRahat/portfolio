About Irfan Sadiq

I’m Irfan Sadiq Rahat, an AI researcher with a B.Tech in CSE (AI & ML) from VIT-AP University. I build models and tools at the intersection of computer vision, knowledge graphs, and safe clinical decision support.

I’ve co-authored 31 publications (510 citations). My practice emphasizes reproducibility (datasets/configs/code), evaluation depth (ROC/AUROC, F1, calibration/ECE, Brier, ablations), and interpretability (Grad-CAM/LIME overlays, case-view tooling). As President of the Machine Learning Club, I scaled a small group into a leading research community through hackathons and reading groups. I’m developing graph-guided, clinician-aligned AI that produces auditable recommendations for resource-constrained settings.

Research Agenda — Built on my past work, growing in scope

Extend reliable image-based systems in agriculture & health with calibrated, explainable models and evidence-tracked knowledge graphs.

Direction 1 — ETHNO-Bridge (ongoing)

Align Traditional Bangladeshi Medicine with modern biomedicine. Built Neo4j graph (~14k nodes, 8.5k edges) linking diseases, drugs, herbs, symptoms, side-effects, diet advice and treatments with evidence. Next: fuse with ICD, SNOMED, DrugBank/DrugCentral, DisGeNET, ClinicalTrials; train graph model to score indications & contraindications; retrieval-guided LM for multi-hop, checkable rationales.

Direction 2 — CARE-GFM

Clinician-Aligned Repositioning Graph Foundation Model using typed graph learning and calibrated losses, with zero/low-shot disease embeddings. Every suggestion points to supporting paths; evaluated via decision-curve and calibration metrics plus expert study. Outputs: Bio-KG, checkpoints, model cards, reusable benchmarks.

Direction 3 — SAFE-Combo

Polypharmacy Graph joining drugs (RxNorm, ATC), diseases/phenotypes (ICD, HPO), genes, pathways, DDIs, side-effects, trial notes, with dose & time tags. Set-based graph model predicts benefit/risk; safety-constrained policy proposes add/stop/swap/dose edits with calibrated uncertainty.
